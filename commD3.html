Discussion Board #3: Listening Skills

Do you consider yourself a good listener? What listening skills do you think are most important? What skills would you like to improve?

I have never consider myself a good speaker even in my native language. However, I believe I am a good listener even if not a perfect one. I don't have great grammar skills plus my limited vocabulary always make me nervous when I try to speak up. Listening does not require to be perfect. I like to listen to people, to concentrate on their subject or problem, try to help them find a right solution. When someone starts a conversation with me, I know that it is necessary to be respectful, pay attention without interruption especially by trying to add a comment or give similar examples. Being a good listener is a process that require patience and ability to pay good attention to the details of the information received. Of course I have a right to agree or disagree with the opinion of other side of conversation, but I should know the details of the opposite side first. In order to become better listener, I should improve my skill by never interrupting or asking questions while someone speaks. Also, I have noticed that it becomes harder to listen when the speaker talks for a very long time about a subject that I am not interested in. In addition, I have hard time listening to long lectures at school. I have to try hard to stay focused and take notes. There is a lot to work on, but I am trying to improve one time at the time, listening to my friends, family, and loved ones.
