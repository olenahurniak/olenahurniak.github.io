Identify the stages of pregnancy

Usually the pregnancy is divided into trimesters. There are three trimesters. In general the normal pregnancy lasts nine months, three trimesters or forty weeks. The first trimester is divided into germinal, embryonic stages and the beginning of the fetal stage. The fetal stage will be to the birth. The germinal stage lasts until the implantation. From the implantation to the eights week the embryonic stage exists. It is the amazing time to the end of which the embryo looks like the small person. It is very important that the development goes in the right way and the all systems work right. The fetal stage is responsible for the development of all organ systems. At 12 -13 days the embryo has a length of 2 mm without body contours. In the 4-week embryo there are cerebral blisters, rudiments of the eyes, ears and mouth, the spinal cord, and later all the central and peripheral nervous systems are formed. The development of all these systems starts very quickly after the conception. Although the nervous system arises at the earliest stages of fetal life, the formation of the brain continues for several years after birth. The heart forms already in the 23rd-day of the embryo. In the process of embryonic development, some tissues or organs of the embryo influence the development of other, placed nearby. This is due to the complex biochemical effects of some parts of the embryo on the other. Although it seems that in the last third stage of a pregnancy, the baby is likely to grow the most, it's a misleading impression. During the first month, the size of the fetus increases by 1000 times. In the second month it increases only by 74 times. In recent months, the rate of development is reduced to 0,3. So the most important stage I believe is the first one, but for sure the whole development of the child from the conception is important.
