Discussion Board #1: Perception: What you see is not always what you get

Task: Briefly describe a time where you were surprised to find out something about someone based on your initial impression or vice versa

I met John at church. I always suspected that he has some kind of disability like Down Syndrome or something else, unknown to me. One day, on John's birthday, his sister told us a story about him. He was born in a big family, which was consisted of 8 children. He grew up as a normal and healthy child. This was a Christian, very religious family who belong to Baptist denomination. It was a hard time for them due to persecution. The government was against this religion. They punished Christians in any possible way they could, so they took all children of this family away from their parents and sent them into different orphanages and organizations. John was sent to a specific organization that "took care of" unwanted children, and used them like rats for experiments and researches in medicine. Each day he was told he is getting vitamins, but truly he was given specific medications they were testing at that time. He was just another variable of an experiment. He does not remember for how much time he spent at that facility and how long he was treated with pills.. He lost the ability to talk normally, and his reflexes got impaired. The government wanted to destroy their faith by dividing the family, by broking them apart from each other. After the persecution was stopped, all siblings got reunited again. They were so happy to see each other, but their brother John was never the same. He went through so much hardship in his life, lost some of the important functions of his body, but he remained strong in his faith in God and the love for his family and loved ones.

Written by

Olena Hurniak, January 16, 2019
