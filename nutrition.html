Identify the difference between "hunger" and "appetite".

Hunger is the physical need of some nutrition elements. When a person does not eat for a long time and he or she is exhausted or just want to eat because the stomach is buzzing. Every person depends on the age and energy spending has some need in the vitamins and minerals and when these needs are not met by the nutrition the organism starts to feel the lack of something and the person wants to eat. As for appetite it is a little more difficult to explain. It is the emotional need of some food, psychological aspect of the nutrition. We can love some kind of food because it arises good feelings in our mind. For example, when we were small, we went to the picnic with our family and eat sandwiches with cheese there, and we remember this day as the happier in our life because we were with our family and there was a lot of fun or we found the puppy in that park and the parents allowed to leave it. Anyway from that time you started to like sandwiches with cheese, when you eat them it gives so much pleasure and good mood and you even do not remember (or do not know) why they are favorite ones. Many food companies profiteer on the appetite. They use in there advertisements or wrappings people who are happy to eat their products. The buyer saw the happy family in the advertisement on TV; they all together smiled and ate pasta. Now standing walking across the store and putting food from the list to the basket the buyer is taking the same pasta because he or she suddenly started to want it, but it was not in the list and there is no need to buy it. An appetite awakens looking at it. And sometimes it is so strong that we cannot fight with it.
